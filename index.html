The challenge

800 pages. Years of SEO equity. Zero room for error.

Migrating a site of this scale is not a copy-paste operation. The real risk is invisible — broken redirect chains, lost canonical tags, schema markup that doesn't carry over, and crawl budget pressure on a site this large.

Most migrations that damage rankings do so silently. The drop only becomes visible in Google Search Console three to four weeks later, when the damage is already done.

The goal here is not just to move the site. It is to move it in a way that Google cannot tell the difference — and where it can, to make the new version stronger.

What could go wrong — and how it gets handled

SEO risks specific to talenttools.com.au

Broken redirects on 800 URLs Mitigated

Every URL is crawled and mapped before migration starts. A redirect spreadsheet is built, tested in staging, and verified with a post-launch crawl using Screaming Frog.

Lost meta titles and descriptions Mitigated

All on-page SEO data is exported pre-migration and re-imported via Rank Math or Yoast, with a verification check across a sample of pages before go-live.

Schema and structured data gaps Flagged

Custom-built sites often use non-standard schema. The audit will identify exactly what is present so it can be rebuilt correctly in WordPress — before go-live, not after.

Crawl budget pressure on a large site Flagged

800 pages requires a clean sitemap, no redirect chains, and correct noindex on admin and utility pages. This is configured as part of the WordPress setup, not an afterthought.
