The Challenge

800 pages. Years of SEO equity. Zero room for error.

Migrating a site of this scale is not a copy-paste operation. The real risk is invisible — broken redirect chains, lost canonical tags, schema markup that doesn't carry over, and crawl budget issues on a site this large. Most migrations that hurt SEO rankings do so silently, and the damage only shows up 3 to 4 weeks later in Google Search Console.

The goal here is not just to move the site. It is to move it in a way that Google cannot tell the difference — and where it can, to make the new version stronger.

What Could Go Wrong — and How It Gets Handled

SEO risks specific to talenttools.com.au

Broken redirects on 800 URLs

Every URL is crawled and mapped before migration starts. A redirect spreadsheet is built, tested in staging, and verified with a post-launch crawl.

Lost meta titles and descriptions

All on-page SEO data is exported pre-migration and re-imported via Rank Math or Yoast with a verification check on a sample of pages.

Schema and structured data gaps

Custom-built sites often use non-standard schema. The audit will flag exactly what is present so it can be rebuilt correctly in WordPress before go-live.

Crawl budget pressure on a large site

800 pages requires a clean sitemap, no redirect chains, and proper noindex on admin pages. This is configured as part of the WordPress setup, not an afterthought.
